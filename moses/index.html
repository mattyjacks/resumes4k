Empowering Africa: The Need for an Africa-Centered Tech System

By Moses Mathu ¬∑ Jul 01, 2023

The Information Age and Social Media Platforms. We find ourselves in the midst of the information age, where information, values, culture, and daily life are easily shareable. The advent of social media platforms such as Facebook, Twitter, and Instagram has played a pivotal role in shaping the way we connect and communicate. However, while these platforms have undoubtedly revolutionized information sharing, there is a concerning aspect that needs to be addressed.

Content Bias: Challenges Faced by Africa. In the context of Africa, social media platforms have created a one-way flow of information. The continent has become a grand recipient of content shared on these platforms, but sadly, matters of little substance often overshadow issues that pose significant challenges to Africa's development. This content bias is fueled by algorithms that determine what receives public attention and what trends. Consequently, content from tech-rich states tends to dominate, while content from tech-poor states, which may be of vital concern, struggles to gain traction.

For example, discussions on topics such as food security in Northern Kenya, political instability in the Democratic Republic of Congo, or civil wars in Sudan often receive minimal attention compared to discussions on LGBTQI+ equality in developed states. It is important to note that this is not to undermine the importance of conversations about sexual orientation, but rather to highlight the disproportionate attention given to certain issues that have little direct impact on Africa's development.

This content bias not only results in poor ranking and attention reception for African content but also raises questions about whether it is a deliberate strategy to divert attention away from Africa's pressing challenges and perpetuate its state of neediness.

Empowering Africa. To overcome these challenges and ensure Africa's empowerment, it is crucial to establish an Africa-centered tech system. By building our own social media platforms with Africanized algorithms, we can take control of our narrative and set ourselves up for autonomy. This would allow us to prioritize and highlight the issues that directly impact our continent's development, ensuring they receive the attention and action they deserve.

By creating platforms that cater to African content, we can foster a more inclusive and representative digital ecosystem. This not only empowers Africans to share their stories, challenges, and solutions but also provides an avenue for global engagement and collaboration. An Africa-centered tech system would enable us to address our prime problems head-on and work towards sustainable development, independent of external content biases.

It is time for Africa to embrace its technological potential, seize control of its digital destiny, and forge a future where African voices and concerns are heard and addressed on equal footing.
